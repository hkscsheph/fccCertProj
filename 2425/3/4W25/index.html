About

Rummikub is one of the most popular game in the world. It was developed by the late Mr. Ephraim Hertzano back in the 50’s. Nowadays Rummikub is sold in over 70 countries and translated into 28 languages.

Lemada’s factory comprises the latest state of the art technology and produces over 3 million educational & developmental games a year. Mostly for export. The head office, includes R&D, Graphic, Import/export and marketing departments. Our factory spreads over 10,000 square meters on 5 acers of land. The factory team, featuring combined experience of more than 50 years, includes: Factory Manager, Manufacturing Engineering, Engineering Maintenance Manager, QC team, Health & Safety Supervisor, Cardboard Division Manager, Injection Molding Division Manager, Logistic Warehouse manager.

The factory is characterized with flexible manufacturing capacity, producing games for both the local market and for Export. We are an eco-friendly company and promotes & maintain a vigorous green production program by using recycled raw material with minimum impact on the environment while turning ideas into profit to allow growth & stability.

History

Mr. Ephraim Hertzano originally made his living selling toothbrushes and other plastic accessories as well as cosmetics. The game started as a brilliant idea that had occurred to Mr. Hertzano, living in Romania, when card-playing was outlawed under the Communist regime.

Mr. Hertzano envisioned playing a game that would use small tiles instead of cards to play, a game that could be played by young and old alike, and that had no ties to any language or religion. He wanted to create a game that would bring people together and one day change the world’s leisure time. He called it Rummikub. Accompanied with a vision just as bright and as grand as the initial idea, it became an overnight hit. The Hertzano family moved to Israel in the 1940’s after World War II, continuing the development on Rummikub in the backyard of his house in Bat Yam, and eventually became a professional game developer after publishing it to the market

How To Play

The game can be played by 2 to 4 people, each of whom draws 14 cards first.

At the beginning, participants must complete the "Ice Breaker", which means that the total number of cards drawn (must comply with the group or sequence rules below) is at least 30. If there is no combination with more than 30 points, you need to draw another card and put it on your own card rack. Please note that you can only use your own cards to form a deck when breaking the ice. You cannot use cards that others have already played on the table. Wild cards can be used to break the ice.

If the players have broken the ice, they can use their cards to form sequences or groups. You can also combine the decks of cards that others have played on the table with your own cards to form a new deck. Each person has 30 seconds (fast), 60 seconds (normal) or 120 seconds (slow) to think each turn. If you cannot play a card, you need to draw a new card.

The person who finishes playing cards the fastest is the winner. If all the covered cards have been taken away but there is no winner, the game ends and the points lost are calculated based on the cards in each person's hand.

Newsroom

Tel-Aviv – May 12, 2024 – Lemada Light Industries today announced a new exclusive collaboration with Hasbro, Inc., a toy and game company. Under the agreement, Hasbro will own distributions rights for Rummikub, the globally popular tile placement game, in North America, in addition to existing distribution rights in France.

“This partnership with Hasbro is a monumental moment for our company and for Rummikub,” said Micha Hertzano, Co-Founder of Rummikub. “Joining forces with a global leader like Hasbro opens endless possibilities and ensures that our game will reach even more players around the world. This collaboration not only validates the hard work we’ve put into our game but also signifies a bright future ahead.”

Rummikub was developed by the late Mr. Ephraim Hertzano back in the 1950’s. Rummikub is currently sold in more than 70 countries and has been translated into 28 languages.

“Adding this beloved game to the Hasbro family is a testament to our commitment to timeless classics that bring families and friends of all ages and generations together for fun and meaningful social interactions,” said Brian Baker, Senior Vice President of Board Games at Hasbro. “We’re excited to welcome Rummikub into our esteemed portfolio, where it will stand proudly alongside other popular games like MONOPOLY, CLUE, OPERATION, and more.”

Rummikub will be released from Hasbro this fall with plans to expand in 2025 and beyond.
